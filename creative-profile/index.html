How we read your answers

The Creative Profile does not score you. It maps you. Every question offers two real ways of working, not a right answer and a wrong one, so there is nothing to game, only a direction to reveal. Your choices gather across the three families that organise the method's eleven habits: how you feed ideas, how you sharpen them, how you protect the work. Your lead type is the orientation your choices keep returning to; your underlying type is the one running just beneath it. Some questions return to the same tension from a new angle, which is how the reading stays honest. What we never do is rank you: there is no top, no percentile, no better type.

You will not find a number, a percentile or a rank anywhere here, and that is deliberate. Creativity is not a quantity you hold more or less of, and treating it like one only teaches people to perform for a scoreboard. So we tell you which kind of creative you are and leave how good you are out of it entirely. The profile is a mirror, not a measurement.

The nine types are our own reading of creative habits, drawn from the method's eleven, offered as a lens, not a lab result, which is why it never hands you a score.
