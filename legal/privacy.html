Privacy policy

Last updated: 17 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Stormridge LLC, operating under the brand Hypercreative ("Hypercreative", "the Company", "we", "us", "our"), handles personal data collected through this website. We keep this short and clear on purpose. If anything is unclear, ask us through the contact form.

Who is responsible for your data

Stormridge LLC (operating as Hypercreative) is the controller of the personal data collected through the website. We decide why and how that data is processed. To reach the team responsible for data protection, use the contact form on the website, or write to us at Stormridge LLC, 30 N Gould St Ste R, Sheridan, WY 82801, United States.

What we collect

We collect only what we need.

Data you give us. When you use the contact form, you choose what to send. This usually includes your name, your work email, your company, your role, and the content of your message. If you share more, we process more, so send only what is relevant.

Data collected automatically. When you visit the website, our systems and our service providers may record technical data such as your IP address, browser type, device type, the pages you view, and the time and date of your visit. Some of this is handled through cookies and similar technology, described in our Cookie Policy.

We do not ask for, and we do not want, special categories of data. Please do not send sensitive personal information through the contact form.

Why we use it

We use personal data for clear and limited purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiry and to follow up on a possible engagement.
  • To run, secure, and improve the website.
  • To understand, in aggregate, how the website is used.
  • To meet our legal and regulatory obligations.

We do not sell your personal data. We do not use it to make decisions about you by automated means with legal or similarly significant effects.

The legal basis

Where the law requires a legal basis, we rely on the following. We process your contact form data because it is necessary to take steps at your request and to pursue our legitimate interest in answering qualified business enquiries. We process technical and analytics data on the basis of our legitimate interest in running a secure and effective website, and, where required, on your consent given through the cookie banner. We process certain data because we are legally obliged to.

How long we keep it

We keep personal data only as long as we need it for the purpose we collected it, and then for any period required to meet legal, accounting, or evidential obligations. Enquiry data that does not lead to an engagement is kept for a reasonable period and then deleted or anonymised. When data is no longer needed, we remove it securely.

Who we share it with

We share personal data only when there is a reason to.

  • Service providers. We work with trusted providers who host the website, send and store communications, and help us analyse usage. They act on our instructions and are bound to protect the data.
  • Professional advisers and authorities. We may disclose data where required by law, regulation, or legal process, or to protect our rights.
  • Business transfers. If the Company is involved in a reorganisation, merger, or sale, data may be transferred as part of that process, subject to this Policy.

We do not share your data with anyone else for their own marketing.

International transfers

Our providers may process data in countries other than your own. Where data is transferred across borders, we rely on appropriate safeguards recognised by applicable law, so that your data keeps the protection described here.

How we protect it

We apply reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against loss, misuse, and unauthorised access. No website or system is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security, but we work to keep risk low and to respond quickly if something goes wrong.

Your rights

Depending on where you are, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correct data that is wrong or incomplete.
  • Ask us to delete your data.
  • Restrict or object to how we process it.
  • Receive your data in a portable format.
  • Withdraw consent where we relied on it, without affecting prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us through the form on the website. We will respond within the time the law allows. We may need to verify your identity first. If you believe we have mishandled your data, you also have the right to complain to your local data protection authority.

Children

The website is for business use by adults. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, tell us through the contact form and we will delete it.

Third party sites

The website may link to other sites we do not control. This Policy does not cover them. Read the privacy policy of any site you visit through a link from ours.

Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. The current version is always the one published here, with its date at the top. Material changes take effect when we publish them.

Contact

For any question about this Policy or about how we handle your data, reach us through the contact form on the website.
